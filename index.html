2011
February

The day we met:

Just as a back story incase you may not know:
Jaimin and I were connected during highschool and dated for a short time until he decided we were too young to take relationships seriously. I still remember the day we first talked and were introduced over the phone by our siblings.

After 8 years with minimal contact we found ourselves at the same place celebrating his best friend’s (Vishal V.) birthday in February 2011. With his charm he planted a seed in my mind that he could be the one I have been searching for all my life. -S.S.

November

Our first date

It was November 2011, Sonal and I hadn’t talked since February of that year due to the fact that Sonal was busy focusing on her CA exams. I got back on to BBM and sent out a broadcast message, which prompted Sonal to reply and see how I was doing. She mentioned that she was so overworked and just needed a break. I recommended that she take a personal day and go to the movies. I was oblivious to her subtle hints of us going together. Until she finally came out and said "how about we go together?"

The next thing I knew we were going to see Sherlock Holmes at Yonge & Eglington and dinner at Grazie across the street. We must have sat there for 3 hours just chatting and catching up over the missed years. I realized since our highschool days we had changed as individuals incredibly.

Truth : I didn’t even realize that this was date, until many months later where she so candidly pointed it out. - J.P.

Our most memorable moment

The Perfect First Kiss.
It was the weekend after Valentine’s Day and I had planned a date for a wine and chocolate tour in Niagara Falls. The drive over was the usual chatting and catching up over the week with no silence for the 2 hour car ride.

A few hours later into our tour, we arrived at our last and favourite vineyard, Pellar Estates. Our tour of the estate had landed off at the gazebo where the sun was setting on the fresh blanket of Canada's white snow ….the rest is history.
-S.S.

May

The first holiday

We decided to take our first trip together, however, Sonal’s one request before this trip was that I meet her parents. Very nervously I had agreed to her request and went to her house for dinner. Little did I know, Dada was over. Oh boy! Did I get the grilling of my life! For anyone who knew Dada, he always had a soft spot for his girls. Quickly seeing the smiles Jaimin put on Sonal's face, Dada could tell he was the one.
Our trip to Cancun, Mexico was our first time away together with the intent of figuring out if I could live with all her craziness...Err I mean our compatibility, to see if we were compatible. Yeah, that's what I meant. “cough, cough”. I’m glad it all worked out ☺. - J.P.

The Proposal

PART ONE.
So I had the ring since September 2013, trying to figure out a perfect way to tell the love of my life to be my wife. I had planned to do it in December 2013, until she ruined the surprised and found out. Now I was in a fix, how was I supposed to make this a surprise? At the perfect timing, she recommended that we head over to Montreal to get away from work. "Cha-ching" we were in business again.

PART TWO. Asking her parents for permission.
Due to the fact that we frequently spent time with her parents at home, this walk in the park (No grilling!). So I went over on the Thursday before heading out to Montreal, put on that same charm that Sonal fell in love with, and asked her parents for her hand in marriage..."Yes". Best part about that conversation was Sonal’s mom’s warning, “I know Sonal can be a little…” and we just left things unsaid as we had mutual understanding of what she was going to say. To celebrate Sonal’s Dad requested that we take a shot of “Jaan”! -J.P.

PART THREE. The day Jaimin asked my parents for permission. Busy at work, I kept getting messages from my sister about going to a friend’s place for dinner. I have never seen her so persistent about getting me to go out for dinner with her. Then I get another call, it’s my mom this time: “Sonal, what time will you be home.” Now I’m annoyed. I get home late in the evening from a long day at work, noticing the washed shot glasses on the drying rack. I ask my dad, what they were for. He says: “Ohhh ..uhhh. Mario was over and we decided to have shots.” I’m thinking okay, this is getting really weird now, something fishy is going on.

PART FOUR. The weekend comes around, we get to our hotel to check-in. The concierge said that the room wasn’t ready and to sit tight for 15mins (she starts speaking in French on the phone). I look at Jaimin, thinking okay whatever, and I realize that he’s got a very weird emotion on his face. For the first time, I see him being nervous. Weird I thought. We get our room and naturally I open the door. I look around and there were candles lite up everywhere in the room, flower petals spread along the floor, and a champagne bottle being chilled. I turn around to Jaimin, saying in mid motion “Wow, this is a really nice hotel, do they do this for everyone”. I fully turned around to see him on one knee…..

In Hinduism, marriage is the most important of the sixteen Sanskars, or sacraments. The Bride and Groom are united spiritually, mentally and physically and enter Grihasthashram, the phase of life devoted to worldly duties such as the creation of home, wealth, and family. The Hindu wedding is a sacred union not simply between Sonal and Jaimin, but between their two souls, and their families. The ceremony you are about to witness consists of a number of rituals, which express the thoughts and emotions of the bride and groom, and both sides of their families.

The ceremony is conducted under a canopy (mandap) symbolizing the universe. The four pillars represent the four parents, and their role in raising the bride and groom. The sacred fire (agni) symbolizes God, and stands as a divine witness to the ceremony.

“Samayu and Pokhan Vidhi” – Welcoming of the Groom

Jaimin and his procession are welcomed by Sonal’s younger sisters and cousins, and are led to the hall. At the doorstep, Jaimin is asked if he is prepared to make a lifelong commitment to Sonal. As this ceremony is performed, the mother of the bride playfully tries to grab Jaimin’s nose, reminding him that he has finally come to their doorstep asking for Sonal’s hand in marriage. Jaimin then breaks an earthen pot, representing the world, which is filled with curd, honey, ghee, and cotton seeds, symbolizing the obstacles he will face and overcome with the help of his bride.

“Var Puja” – Paying Respect to the Groom

Jaimin and his family proceed to the mandap, where the bride’s parents offer him a seat, water, and madhuparka- a sweet mixture of yoghurt, honey, and purified butter-symbolizing the sweetness of the wedding ceremony and the bride and groom’s future life together. The bride’s parents wash the feet of the groom, and thus welcoming him.

”Kanya Agman” – Arrival of the Bride

Sonal is escorted to the mandap by her maternal uncle (mama). The bride is seated across form the groom, whose view of her is blocked by a cloth curtain (Antarpath), held by Sonal’s brothers. A sacred, eight-verse hymn (mangalashtak) is chanted, invoking Gods, Goddesses, and natural elements, to witness and bless the union of the couple. When the recitation is over, the Antarpath is removed, and Jaimin and Sonal welcome each other with fresh flower garlands (haars), symbolizing that they have chosen each other as life long companions.

“Varmala” – Cotton Garland

A loop of white cotton thread, wound 24 times (varmala), which symbolizes different characteristics and virtues of human life, is placed around the shoulders of Jaimin and Sonal. The threads bind them together, so that they will fulfill their roles as husband and wife together, fully and sincerely. A single thread is easily broken, but many together form a very strong bond. In the same way, the couple’s virtues and abilities bind them together securely.

“Kanya Daan” – Giving away of the Bride

At this stage of the ceremony, Sonal’s parents perform rituals symbolizing the giving away of their daughter. This is considered one of the holiest and most valuable sacrifices (daan) that Sonal’s parents could ever make.

“Hasta Melap” – Joining of Hands

Sonal’s right hand is placed in Jaimin’s right hand, and blessed with holy water, symbolizing the joining of their hearts and their acceptance of each other as partners for life.

“Granthi Bandhan” – Tying of the Nuptial Knot

The sacred knot is tied with the outer garments of the bride and groom. This knot symbolizes the bond of marriage and the couple’s love and everlasting relationship.

“Agni Puja” – Invoking the Sacred Fire

The fire (agni) is invoked in the centre of the mandap, and the God of Fire (Agni devta) is invited to witness and bless the union between Jaimin and Sonal. Fire is a purifying agents as well as a source of energy. Fire also represents knowledge and illumination of the mind.

“Lajja Homa” – Offerings to the Fire

Sonal’s brother(s) give the couple rice, oats and leaves to offer to the fire. This represents the couple’s willingness to sacrifice worldly possessions for the blessings of God.

“Mangal Fera” – Circling the Holy Fire

Sonal and Jaimin will circle the fire four times while holy hymns (mantras) are chanted. The four circles are symbolic of life’s attributes and the vows are taken as follows:

Dharma- I shall lead us on the path of righteousness

Artha- I shall lead us towards prosperity

Kaama- I shall lead us towards our desires

Moksha- I shall lead us towards spiritual upliftment and salvation.

“Shilarohana Vidhi” – Touching of the Stone

While circling the sacred fire, the bride and groom will each touch their right foot to a stone to show that they will be as firm as a rock when facing life’s hardships.

“Saptapadi” – Seven Sacred Vows

This is a very important part of the ceremony, where the bride and the groom walk seven steps together. It is Hindu philosophy that if people walk seven steps together, they become life long friends living in unison as soul mates. These are the seven steps taken together both affirming and consenting to the seven vows during the marital life.
